Let's be honest: most corporate wellness programs are window dressing.

A fruit basket in the kitchen. A one-off yoga session. A reminder to use the Employee Assistance Program that nobody talks about. These are gestures, not culture.

## What actually works

After navigating my own health challenges in a corporate environment, I've come to believe that genuine wellness culture has three non-negotiable ingredients:

**1. Psychological safety** — People need to feel that talking about mental health, asking for accommodations, or admitting they're struggling won't be used against them.

**2. Manager capability** — Line managers have an enormous impact on team well-being. Investing in their ability to have honest, empathetic conversations is one of the highest-leverage things an organisation can do.

**3. Structural support** — Flexible working, reasonable workloads, access to quality health resources. Wellness can't be an individual's responsibility if the system itself is the source of stress.

## The individual piece

Even in imperfect environments, individuals can build resilience and well-being practices that help them weather the storms. That's where coaching comes in — not as a substitute for systemic change, but as a practical support right now.
