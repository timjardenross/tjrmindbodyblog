Chronic pain is one of the most prevalent and under-discussed health challenges in Australia today.

## Prevalence

Approximately 3.6 million Australians — around 1 in 5 adults — live with chronic pain. It is more common than heart disease, diabetes and cancer combined, yet receives a fraction of the research funding and public awareness.

## Who is affected?

Chronic pain does not discriminate. It affects people across all age groups, though prevalence increases with age. Women are slightly more likely to experience chronic pain than men, and people in lower socioeconomic groups face a disproportionate burden due to limited access to specialist care.

## The hidden cost

Beyond the physical toll, chronic pain carries enormous psychological and social costs. Depression and anxiety are significantly more common in people with chronic pain, and many experience isolation, reduced ability to work, and strained relationships.

## A path forward

The good news is that a holistic, multi-dimensional approach — addressing not just the physical pain but the mental, emotional, and social dimensions of well-being — has been shown to significantly improve quality of life for people living with chronic conditions.

If you're navigating chronic pain, know that you are not alone, and that support is available.
